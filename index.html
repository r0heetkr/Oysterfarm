Our Story

Grown in the dark,
delivered in the light

We started Oyster Farm in a 400 sq ft converted shed with four fruiting shelves and a dream. Today we operate a 2,000 sq ft climate-controlled grow room producing fresh oyster mushrooms year-round for restaurants, farmers markets, and home cooks across the region.

Every cluster is grown on pasteurised wheat straw and hardwood sawdust — no pesticides, no growth hormones, no compromises. From spawn to harvest in under three weeks.

Why Oyster Mushrooms

The most versatile fungus
in the kitchen

🍳

Cooks in minutes

Oyster mushrooms need just 3–5 minutes in a hot pan. Their broad caps caramelise beautifully — a flavour far deeper than the time suggests.

💪

Nutritional powerhouse

High in protein, B-vitamins, potassium, and beta-glucans. A genuine meat alternative that earns its place on the plate rather than just standing in.

🌍

Lightest footprint

Grown on agricultural waste, needing no soil, very little water, and zero sunlight. Oyster mushrooms are among the most sustainable foods on earth.

How We Grow

From spore to
your kitchen

Every batch follows the same careful sequence — no shortcuts, no rushing the mycelium. That discipline is why our mushrooms consistently taste like the forest, not the factory.

1

Substrate preparation

We mix wheat straw and hardwood sawdust, then pasteurise at 80 °C for 12 hours to eliminate competing moulds — no chemical sterilisation.

2

Inoculation

Mushroom spawn (grain colonised with mycelium) is mixed through the cooled substrate inside a sterile flow hood. Each bag is sealed and labelled.

3

Colonisation — 14 days

Bags rest in a dark room at 22 °C while the mycelium threads through the substrate, turning it white. No light, no misting — just time.

4

Fruiting — 5 to 7 days

Bags move to our fruiting chamber: 16 °C, 90% humidity, 12 hours of light daily. Pins emerge within 48 hours and reach full size in under a week.

5

Hand harvest & delivery

Each cluster is twisted off by hand at peak ripeness — caps still curled inward. Packed in paper, never plastic, and delivered within 24 hours.
